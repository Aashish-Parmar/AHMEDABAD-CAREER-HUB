# üíº Recruiter Role - User Guide & Explanation

## üìã Overview

The **Recruiter Role** is designed for HR professionals and recruiters who want to find and hire talented students and professionals. As a recruiter on the Ahmedabad Career Hub platform, you can create company profiles, post job openings, manage job listings, review and manage applications from candidates, track application statuses, and build your company's presence in the job market.

---

## üöÄ Getting Started

### **Registration**

To get started as a recruiter, you need to create an account:

1. **Navigate to Registration Page** - Click on "Register" from the home page or login page
2. **Select Recruiter Role** - Choose "Recruiter" when prompted to select your role
3. **Company Selection** - You have two options:
   - **Option A: Select Existing Company** - If your company is already registered:
     - Use the searchable dropdown to find your company
     - Search by company name
     - Select your company from the list
   - **Option B: Create New Company** - If your company doesn't exist:
     - You'll need to create the company first (see Company Management section)
     - Then register as a recruiter linked to that company
4. **Fill Required Information:**
   - **Name**: Your full name (minimum 2 characters)
   - **Email**: A valid email address (must be unique)
   - **Password**: Must be at least 8 characters with:
     - At least one uppercase letter
     - At least one lowercase letter
     - At least one number
   - **Company**: Selected from dropdown (or company ID if creating separately)
5. **Submit** - Your account will be created and you'll be automatically logged in

### **Login**

After registration, you can login anytime using:
- Your registered email address
- Your password

You'll stay logged in across sessions until you manually logout.

### **2. Company Management**

Create and manage your company profile:

#### **Create Company**

**When to Use:**
- First time setting up your company on the platform
- Before registering as a recruiter (if company doesn't exist)

**Information Needed:**
- **Company Name** - Official company name (must be unique)
- **Description** - Detailed description of your company, what you do, culture, values

**How to Create:**
1. Navigate to company creation (can be done during registration or separately)
2. Fill in all required fields

- Provide detailed company description to attract better candidates
- Include all relevant technologies in tech stack
- Use professional logo image
- Keep information up to date

### **3. Post Job** (`/post-job`)

Create and publish job openings:

**What You Need:**
- **Job Title** - Clear, descriptive title (e.g., "Full Stack Developer", "React Intern")
- **Job Description** - Comprehensive description including:
  - Role and responsibilities
  - Requirements and qualifications
  - What you're looking for in candidates
  - Company benefits (optional)
- **Job Type** - Select either:
  - **Internship** - For internship positions
  - **Full-time** - For permanent positions
- **Salary/Stipend** - Compensation details
  - Can be a range (e.g., "‚Çπ50,000 - ‚Çπ80,000")
  - Can be fixed amount (e.g., "‚Çπ15,000/month")
  - Be transparent about compensation
- **Location** - Where the job is located
  - Can be specific city (e.g., "Ahmedabad")
  - Can be "Remote" for remote positions
  - Can be "Hybrid" for hybrid work
- **Required Skills** - List of skills/technologies required
  - Enter as comma-separated list (e.g., "React, Node.js, MongoDB")
  - Be specific but realistic
  - Include both technical and soft skills if relevant

**How to Post:**
1. Navigate to "Post Job" from dashboard or navigation menu
2. Fill in all required fields
3. Write a detailed, compelling job description
4. Select appropriate job type
5. Enter salary/stipend information
6. Specify location
7. Add required skills (comma-separated)
8. Review all information
9. Click "Post Job" to publish
10. You'll see a success confirmation

**Best Practices:**
- Write clear, detailed job descriptions
- Be specific about requirements
- Include information about company culture
- Set realistic expectations
- Use proper formatting for readability
- Include keywords relevant to the role

### **5. Manage Applications** (`/manage-applications`)

Review and manage applications from candidates:

**This is one of the most important features for recruiters!**

#### **View Applications**

**Features:**
- **Filter by Job** - View applications for a specific job posting
- **Filter by Status** - Filter applications by current status:
  - **Applied** - New applications just received
  - **Reviewed** - Applications you've reviewed
  - **Shortlisted** - Candidates selected for further rounds
  - **Rejected** - Candidates not selected
  - **Hired** - Successfully hired candidates
- **View All Company Applications** - See all applications across all jobs
- **Pagination** - Navigate through multiple pages of applications

**Application Information Displayed:**
- **Applicant Details:**
  - Name
  - Email
  - College (for students)
  - Profile picture/avatar
- **Job Information:**
  - Job title
  - Link to view full job details
- **Application Details:**
  - Application date
  - Current status
- **Actions:**
  - Update status dropdown

#### **Update Application Status**

**Status Workflow:**
1. **Applied** ‚Üí Candidate has submitted application (default status)
2. **Reviewed** ‚Üí You've reviewed the application
3. **Shortlisted** ‚Üí Candidate is selected for interview/next round
4. **Rejected** ‚Üí Candidate is not selected
5. **Hired** ‚Üí Candidate has been hired for the position

**How to Manage:**
1. Navigate to "Manage Applications" from dashboard
2. Select a job from dropdown (optional - view all if not selected)
3. Optionally filter by status
4. Review each application:
   - Read applicant information
   - Check their profile details
   - View the job they applied to
5. Update status using the dropdown:
   - Select new status from dropdown
   - Status updates automatically
   - You'll see a confirmation message
6. Navigate through pages if you have many applications

**Best Practices:**
- Review applications promptly
- Update status as you progress through the hiring process
- Keep statuses current so candidates know where they stand
- Use "Reviewed" status to track which applications you've looked at
- Shortlist promising candidates early
- Mark hired candidates appropriately

**Tips:**
- Regularly check for new applications
- Respond to candidates in a timely manner
- Maintain organized application tracking
- Use status filtering to focus on specific stages

## üîë Key Workflows

### **Workflow 1: Posting a Job and Managing Applications**

1. **Post Job** - Create and publish a job opening with all details
2. **Monitor Applications** - Check "Manage Applications" regularly for new candidates
3. **Review Applications** - Read through candidate applications and profiles
4. **Update Status** - Move candidates through the hiring pipeline:
   - Applied ‚Üí Reviewed ‚Üí Shortlisted ‚Üí Hired

5. **Track Progress** - Use status filters to see candidates at each stage

1. **Create Company** - Set up your company profile with all information
2. **Register as Recruiter** - Create recruiter account linked to company
3. **Complete Profile** - Update your recruiter profile
4. **Post First Job** - Start posting job openings
5. **Manage Brand** - Ensure company information is accurate and appealing

### **Workflow 3: Active Recruitment**

1. **Post Multiple Jobs** - Create postings for various open positions
2. **Review Applications Daily** - Check for new applications regularly
3. **Shortlist Candidates** - Identify promising candidates
4. **Interview Process** - Conduct interviews (outside platform)
5. **Update Statuses** - Keep application statuses current
6. **Hire Candidates** - Mark successful hires
7. **Close Filled Positions** - Remove filled job postings

## üí° Best Practices for Recruiters

### **Job Postings:**

1. **Write Clear Descriptions**
   - Be specific about role requirements
   - Include responsibilities clearly
   - Mention company culture and benefits
   - Set realistic expectations

2. **Use Appropriate Job Types**
   - Correctly mark internships vs. full-time positions
   - Set appropriate salary/stipend expectations

3. **Specify Skills Accurately**
   - List actual required skills
   - Don't over-specify (unnecessarily limit candidates)
   - Include both technical and soft skills if needed

4. **Keep Postings Current**
   - Update or remove filled positions
   - Refresh job descriptions periodically
   - Remove outdated postings

### **Application Management:**

1. **Respond Promptly**
   - Review applications in a timely manner
   - Update statuses as you progress
   - Don't leave candidates waiting

2. **Be Organized**
   - Use status filtering effectively
   - Keep track of where each candidate is in the process
   - Maintain clear communication through status updates

3. **Fair Evaluation**
   - Review all applications fairly
   - Give every candidate proper consideration
   - Update statuses accurately

4. **Maintain Professionalism**
   - Keep application statuses updated
   - Provide timely feedback through status changes
   - Maintain good candidate experience

### **Company Profile:**

1. **Complete Information**
   - Fill all company details
   - Write compelling company description
   - Include all relevant technologies

2. **Keep It Updated**
   - Update company information as needed
   - Refresh tech stack when technologies change
   - Maintain current address and contact info

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I post multiple jobs?**
A: Yes! You can post as many job openings as needed for your company.

**Q: Can I edit a job after posting?**
A: Currently, you can delete and repost jobs. Job editing functionality may be available in future updates.

**Q: How do candidates see my job postings?**
A: All posted jobs are publicly visible on the Jobs page where students can browse and apply.

**Q: How will I know when someone applies?**
A: Applications appear in your "Manage Applications" page. Check regularly for new applications with "Applied" status.

**Q: Can I see candidate contact information?**
A: Yes, when viewing applications, you can see candidate name, email, and college information.

**Q: What happens when I update an application status?**
A: The status updates immediately and candidates can see their updated status in their "My Applications" page.

**Q: Can multiple recruiters from the same company use the platform?**
A: Yes, multiple recruiters can register and be linked to the same company. Each recruiter can manage jobs and applications.

**Q: How do I delete a job?**
A: Go to Recruiter Dashboard, find the job, and click delete. You'll be asked to confirm before deletion.

**Q: Can I see application statistics?**
A: Application counts and statistics may be available in future updates. Currently, you can view applications with filtering options.

**Q: What if I need to change my company information?**
A: Company editing functionality may be available in future updates. Contact support if urgent changes are needed.

**Q: How long do job postings stay active?**
A: Job postings stay active until you delete them. It's recommended to remove filled or outdated positions.

**Q: Can I view applications from candidates who applied to jobs posted by other recruiters in my company?**
A: Yes, if you filter by "All Company Applications", you'll see applications for all jobs posted by your company.
